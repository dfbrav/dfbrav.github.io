Hi! Welcome to my website !

My name is David Lankford-Bravo and I am a geoscientist passionate about integrating technology and field work to better understand our earth, its history, and its processes. Feel free to explore this website to learn more about my projects and collaborations!           

Who I am and Who I aim to be

Creative,  Curious, Driven, Collaborative and Dynamic

I am a values-driven scientist, student, and leader focusing working to support the people, teams and systems around me. I believe that living a balanced life is critical to functioning at the highest level and actively strive to better balance an active lifestyle with one of study and research.  Having grown up half-hispanic, access to education and cultural inclusion are very important to me as is supporting diversity of thought. Though I have many hobbies, biking, hiking, camping, photography and language learning are my most active ones and you'll see some of my favorite pictures from my travels in the photography tab. Click around this website and its various tabs to see some of the 3D models I've developed for my PhD research, learn more about my involvement with the World Petroleum Council, and explore the fun work I've done with collaborators and our mentees!             

Professional life at a glance     

      

Science 

Through research focused on salt tectonics, plate tectonics, geophysical forward modelling and integrating data science and analytics with field geology I aim to contribute our every growing energy and environmental science     body of knowledge. I'm fascinated with integrating technology with fied geology and actively work on developing new workflows to integrate the power of photogrammetry with the analysis of detailed geologic work. You can look at my CV to see an up to date list of my work.

Extracurriculars 

I focus on professional society extra curriculars aimed at education and innovation. I currently serve on the World Petroleum Council's Young Professionals Committee where I contribute to their mission of promoting the use of our energy resources for the good of all as a young professional representative of the US National Committee to the World Petroleum Council.                   

Mentorship

Through multiple collaborations with Sarah M. Giles I have helped create, fund, and mentor multiple undergraduate student projects with the goal of exposing students to the joys of research, presenting at scientific meetings, and preparing for a career applying their scientific knowledge. Check out the collaborations tab to learn more about my collaborations and our students past and present.

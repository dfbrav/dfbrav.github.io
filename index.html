Introduction

Hi there! My name is David Lankford-Bravo and I am currently a PhD student at the University of Texas at El Paso. My PhD thesis is focused on understanding the development of salt structures around the Onion Creek Salt Diapir in Fisher Valley, Utah. I use traditional field geology methods coupled with modern technology to make observations about geology that otherwise would be unattainable. I'm also focused on trying to bring datascience and machine learning to field geology. My github repository will host some of those efforts in python.

I'm also really interested in communicating and teaching earth science in a way that makes our field more accessible to researchers and students. One way I do that is by building 3D photogrammetric models of outcrops using drones. I then integrate the field-collected data into the model to visualize the relationships we see.
